Risk Level

Headaches

Risk of Headaches is Elevated when AQI is Poor (101–150). Fine particles and NO₂ irritate the nervous system, triggering tension and migraine events.

Common Signs
Forehead pressure Temple tightness Light sensitivity Smell sensitivity Fatigue Difficulty concentrating

✦ Do's

  • Choose indoor clean air environments
  • Stay well hydrated throughout the day
  • Keep prescribed migraine meds accessible

✦ Don'ts

  • Avoid intense outdoor exercise on high AQI days
  • Don't ignore worsening or unusual symptoms
👁️
⚡ Moderate Trigger Risk
Risk Level

Eye Irritation

Risk is Moderate when AQI is Moderate (101–200). Particulate matter and ozone irritate the conjunctiva, causing inflammation and discomfort.

Common Signs
Redness Watering eyes Burning sensation Blurred vision Itching

✦ Do's

  • Wear wraparound glasses outdoors
  • Use lubricating eye drops regularly
  • Rinse eyes with clean water after going outside

✦ Don'ts

  • Avoid rubbing eyes outdoors
  • Don't wear contact lenses on high AQI days

🔴 High Trigger Risk
Risk Level

Pregnancy & Infants

Risk is High during poor air quality days. Ultrafine particles cross the placental barrier, linked to reduced fetal oxygen and low birth weight outcomes.

Common Signs
Reduced fetal oxygen Infant respiratory distress Premature birth risk Low birth weight

✦ Do's

  • Stay indoors as much as possible
  • Use air purifier in sleeping area
  • Consult doctor if unusual symptoms occur

✦ Don'ts

  • Avoid heavy traffic and construction areas
  • Don't exercise outdoors when AQI is Moderate+
💨
🔴 High Trigger Risk
Risk Level

Asthma

Risk of attacks is High when AQI is Poor or above. Airborne triggers constrict airways and increase bronchial inflammation in sensitive individuals.

Common Signs
Wheezing Shortness of breath Chest tightness Increased inhaler use Night coughing

✦ Do's

  • Keep rescue inhaler accessible at all times
  • Use air purifier indoors
  • Wear N95 mask outdoors

✦ Don'ts

  • Avoid outdoor exercise when AQI is Poor+
  • Don't skip preventive medication
❤️
⚡ Elevated Trigger Risk
Risk Level

Heart Issues

Risk is Elevated on poor AQI days. PM2.5 enters the bloodstream causing systemic inflammation, increased clotting risk, and arterial stress.

Common Signs
Chest pain Palpitations Elevated blood pressure Shortness of breath Fatigue

✦ Do's

  • Monitor blood pressure more frequently
  • Stay indoors during peak pollution hours
  • Keep heart medication accessible

✦ Don'ts

  • Avoid strenuous outdoor activity
  • Don't ignore chest tightness or breathlessness
🌿
⚡ Moderate Trigger Risk
Risk Level

Allergies

Risk of reactions is Moderate during elevated AQI. Pollutants amplify the immune response to allergens, intensifying histamine reactions.

Common Signs
Sneezing Runny nose Skin rashes Itching Watery eyes Hives

✦ Do's

  • Keep antihistamines handy
  • Shower after coming indoors
  • Keep windows closed on high pollen/AQI days

✦ Don'ts

  • Avoid outdoor morning walks during high AQI
  • Don't dry clothes outside on bad air days
🫁
⚡ Elevated Trigger Risk
Risk Level

Sinus

Risk of sinus issues is Elevated when AQI is poor. Particulates and chemical irritants inflame the sinus passages causing chronic congestion and pressure.

Common Signs
Nasal congestion Facial pressure Headache Post-nasal drip Reduced smell

✦ Do's

  • Use saline nasal spray to clear passages
  • Stay well hydrated
  • Use a humidifier indoors

✦ Don'ts

  • Avoid smoky or dusty environments
  • Don't skip prescribed nasal medication

Disclaimer: The health risks above are precautionary recommendations based on current AQI levels. This is not medical advice. Please consult a clinician if you experience severe or worsening symptoms.
